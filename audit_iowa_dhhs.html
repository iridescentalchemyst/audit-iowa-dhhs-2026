What This Is

The Case for an Independent Audit

Since 2003, the federal government has reviewed Iowa's child welfare system four times through the Child and Family Services ReviewA federal report card for child welfareThe CFSR is conducted by the Administration for Children and Families (ACF). States are assessed on safety, permanency, and well-being outcomes under Title IV-B and IV-E of the Social Security Act.Etymology"Review" from Latin revidere — to see again. Iowa has been seen again, and again, and again.. Iowa has never achieved full conformityMeeting the federal standard — Iowa never hasUnder CFSR, "conformity" requires a performance threshold of 95% or higher on most measures. Iowa has not achieved full conformity in any completed round.EtymologyFrom Latin conformare — to form together. Iowa's child welfare system has never been shaped alike with federal standards in over two decades of review. in any of those four rounds. Not once.

This petition asks Iowa Auditor of State Rob Sand to conduct an independent performance auditAn investigation into whether government is actually workingA performance audit evaluates whether a government agency is achieving its objectives in accordance with applicable laws and standards. Governed by GAGAS — Generally Accepted Government Auditing Standards.Etymology"Performance" from Latin performare — to carry through completely. A performance audit asks: did you carry it through? under Iowa Code § 11.6(3) and § 11.24.

The Analytical Framework

Domains & Deficiency Categories

Every attachment, finding, and audit question is coded to the eight analytical domains (A–H) and thirteen deficiency categories (F1–F13) that structure the petition's analysis.

Eight Analytical Domains

A
Funding Authorization & Federal Compliance

Federal and state child welfare funding streams, reimbursement compliance, budget allocation, and financial oversight controls.

B
Contract & Provider Oversight

Oversight, monitoring, enforcement, and accountability mechanisms governing contracted providers and service delivery entities.

C
Decision Controls & Case Authorization

Policies, decision-support tools, and controls governing case actions, removals, placements, service authorization, and ICWA compliance.

D
Judicial Interface & Legal Oversight

Agency-court interaction, evidentiary submissions, guardian ad litem coordination, ICWA tribal notification, and oversight mechanisms.

E
Outcomes, Incentives & Systemic Disparities

Measured child welfare outcomes, performance incentives, demographic disparities, and operational metrics including safety, permanency, and well-being tracking.

F
Workforce Capacity & Internal Accountability

Workforce stability, staffing, supervision systems, training adequacy, internal accountability, and ICWA-specific training requirements.

G
Federal Oversight History & Corrective Action Response

CFSR cycles, PIPs, OIG findings, DOJ investigations, corrective action implementation, and patterns of recurring findings across review cycles.

H
Data Integrity & System Auditability

Reliability, accessibility, auditability, and governance of administrative and case management data systems — including audit trails and reporting infrastructure.

F5 is the system failure multiplier — present wherever prior findings went uncorrected. F10 is the catastrophic failure marker — present in child fatality and severe injury patterns. F13 is the systemic exposure flag — present wherever policy compliance on paper conceals operational non-performance.

23 Years of Federal Oversight

A Longitudinal Record

2003
CFSR Round 1 — Statewide Assessment & Final Report

Iowa achieves substantial conformity with only 2 of 7 outcomes and 3 of 7 systemic factors. PIP required. Caseloads documented at 35–55 per worker; caseworker visit compliance at 10% for children.

2010–11
CFSR Round 2 — Statewide Assessment & Final Report

Second federal review cycle. Iowa again fails full conformity. The same systemic factors from Round 1 reappear. A second PIP is required.

2016–17
Child Fatality Investigations: Finn, Ray, Duis

Independent investigations find Iowa DHS involvement prior to each fatal outcome. Documented deficiencies: F1, F2, F3, F10, F12 — across all three cases.

2018
CFSR Round 3 — Statewide Assessment & Final Report

Third federal review. Non-conformity persists. A third PIP is required. F5 (Repeat Findings Not Corrected) now documented across all three completed rounds.

2019–21
SDM Implementation — Safety Assessment Modernization

Iowa's own internal review identifies the legacy safety assessment tool as producing inconsistent decisions. New SDM instrument (RC-0104) deployed with Evident Change. Published December 2021.

2023
SDM Post-Implementation Analysis

84% accuracy rate documented across ~250,000 safety assessments. Improvement over legacy tool — but roughly 1 in 6 decisions assessed as inaccurate. F1 persists into the modernized tool era.

Feb 2026
Iowa VISION System Declared "National Model"

ACF and Governor Reynolds jointly announce Iowa's CCWIS VISION system as a national model. The announcement does not reference Iowa's CFSR conformity status or Domain H data integrity gaps.

Jun 2026
ASPE Report & ACF Industry Day

ASPE publishes finding that fewer than ⅓ of CCWIS projects nationally are operational after $2B in federal investment. Petitioner participates in ACF Child Welfare Technology Modernization Industry Day.

2026
Petition Submitted to Iowa Auditor of State

20 sections, 66+ attachments, three companion documents. Submitted to Auditor Rob Sand under Iowa Code § 11.6(3) and § 11.24. Final preparation in progress.

Ashley Marie Meredith is a registered nurse with a Master of Science in Nursing and the founder of Iowa Family Rights.
